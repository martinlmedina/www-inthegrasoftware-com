Using the full Inthegra Software website context, generate the mockup concept for the People & Culture page.

Strategic objective:
This page must work primarily as an institutional trust-building page, not as a recruiting landing page.

It should communicate that behind Inthegra’s products, solutions and enterprise implementations there is a strong, experienced, collaborative and human team.

Important positioning:
- This is NOT a startup culture page
- This is NOT mainly a hiring page
- This is NOT a generic HR page
- This IS an institutional page that reinforces trust, maturity, execution capability and company identity

The page must balance two dimensions:
1. Human warmth and internal culture
2. Professional seniority, delivery mindset and enterprise credibility

The tone should communicate:
- Serious company
- Strong teams
- Technology + business understanding
- Collaborative culture
- Professional maturity
- Long-term vision
- Execution capability
- Human closeness without looking informal

Core message to transmit:
Inthegra’s culture is not decorative.
It is part of how the company delivers complex software, works with clients, scales teams and sustains long-term relationships.

The page should make clear that:
- Inthegra has more than 20 years of trajectory
- The team works on real enterprise challenges
- The company combines technology expertise with business understanding
- Collaboration, communication and responsibility are part of delivery quality
- Team capability is one of the reasons clients can trust Inthegra

Required sections:

1. Hero section
   - Strong institutional headline around people, culture and execution
   - Subheadline that connects team quality with delivery quality
   - Premium visual treatment using real team imagery if available
   - CTA should be institutional, not recruiting-first
   - Example CTA directions:
     - Conocé Inthegra
     - Hablemos
     - Trabajá con un equipo que entiende tu negocio

2. Who we are
   - Brief story of Inthegra as a company
   - Human but credible tone
   - Show trajectory, commitment, evolution and identity
   - This section should create emotional connection without losing corporate seriousness

3. How we work
   - This is one of the most important sections
   - Show real working principles, not generic motivational phrases
   - Examples of ideas:
     - multidisciplinary collaboration
     - product + services mindset
     - communication with context
     - documentation and clarity
     - continuous evolution
     - focus on client reality
   - Make it feel operational and real

4. Principles translated into action
   - Reframe company values so they feel concrete
   - Avoid cliché “values cards” with vague copy
   - Show how principles appear in daily work and project execution
   - Examples:
     - ownership
     - clarity
     - collaboration
     - continuous improvement
     - business understanding
     - long-term thinking

5. Team capability / expertise section
   - Show that team quality is part of the product and service quality
   - This section should feel enterprise-grade
   - Possible themes:
     - senior talent
     - Oracle expertise
     - real implementation experience
     - cross-functional knowledge
     - growth through projects
   - Important:
     do not over-focus on hierarchy or leadership portraits unless they add trust

6. Culture and human side
   - Add warmth, energy and authenticity
   - Use real images, moments, internal experiences, celebrations, collaboration scenes
   - This section should support employer branding but still feel aligned with the institutional goal
   - Avoid looking childish or startup-like
   - More “human professional company” than “fun office culture”

7. Optional future / careers block
   - This section should be secondary, not dominant
   - It can invite talent to connect, but without turning the page into a CV landing page
   - Better approach:
     “Si te interesa construir tecnología con impacto real, conversemos”
   - Avoid heavy recruiting mechanics on this page

8. Final CTA
   - Must close with trust and connection
   - Should work both for potential clients and potential talent
   - It must feel premium, calm and confident

Design and visual direction:
- Premium institutional aesthetic
- Clean enterprise layout
- High-quality real photography preferred
- Strong typography and spacious composition
- Avoid generic stock-HR aesthetics
- Avoid overusing smiling posed office shots
- If visual support graphics are used, incorporate the existing Inthegra background graphics subtly so they enrich the composition without affecting readability
- Motion and interactions should feel elegant, restrained and modern

Important content guidance:
- Do not make the page feel like a blog
- Do not make it feel like a jobs portal
- Do not make it feel too cold or too corporate
- Do not make it feel like any generic software company could use it
- It must feel specifically like Inthegra:
  experienced, serious, human, collaborative and capable of delivering enterprise software
